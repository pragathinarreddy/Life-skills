# Listening and Assertive Communication

## 1. Active Listening

### Question 1: Steps/Strategies for Active Listening
- Maintain eye contact
- Avoid interrupting
- Focus fully on the speaker
- Ask clarifying questions
- Paraphrase what the speaker said
- Observe tone and body language

## 2. Reflective Listening

### Question 2: Key Points of Reflective Listening
- Listen carefully without interrupting
- Repeat the message in your own words
- Confirm understanding with the speaker
- Notice emotions and body language
- Clarify doubts immediately
- Ensure both sides agree on the message

## 3. Reflection

### Question 3: Obstacles in My Listening Process
- Thinking about my reply while others speak
- Getting distracted easily
- Making assumptions
- Losing focus in long discussions

### Question 4: How I Can Improve My Listening
- Keep distractions away
- Take notes during meetings
- Ask questions for clarity
- Be fully present in conversations

## 4. Types of Communication

### Question 5: When Do I Use Passive Communication?
- When I want to avoid conflict
- When I feel unsure about my opinion

### Question 6: When Do I Use Aggressive Communication?
- When I feel frustrated
- When deadlines are near

### Question 7: When Do I Use Passive-Aggressive Communication?
- When I feel upset but don’t express it directly
- When I use sarcasm instead of being clear

### Question 8: How Can I Make My Communication Assertive?
- Use “I” statements
- Speak calmly and clearly
- Express disagreement respectfully
- Listen before responding
- Set clear boundaries
